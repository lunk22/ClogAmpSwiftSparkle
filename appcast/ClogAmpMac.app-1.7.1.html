1.7.1

  • Audio Compression
    A dynamic compressor can now be enabled in Settings > General to reduce the volume difference between loud and quiet passages during playback.

    How to enable: Go to Settings > General and check "Enable Compression". Then reload the currently loaded song for the change to take effect.

    Parameters:
    • Threshold (dB): The volume level above which compression kicks in. A lower value (e.g. -24 dB) means more of the audio gets compressed. Default: -18 dB.
    • Head Room (dB): Controls how gently the compression is applied. A higher value results in softer, more gradual compression. Default: 5 dB.
    • Master Gain (dB): Makeup gain to restore overall volume after compression has reduced the louder parts. Increase this if the song sounds too quiet after compression. Default: 0 dB.

    Click the info button (?) next to the compression settings for a quick reference of these parameters.

  • Improved Audio Normalization for stereo files
    The normalization algorithm now correctly analyzes both channels of stereo files and uses the peak of the louder channel to calculate the gain adjustment, preventing clipping on either side.

  • Clock Window improvements
    The title bar and traffic light buttons have been removed for a cleaner look. Additionally, the clock window can now become fully transparent when it is not being hovered with the mouse. Enable this in Settings > General with the "Transparent when inactive" option. When you move the mouse over the clock window it returns to its normal appearance; move the mouse away and it fades back to transparent.
